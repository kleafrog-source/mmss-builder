"Thaas theory" is not a standard scientific term; the search results show possible misinterpretations, including the De Haas–Van Alphen (DHVA) effect (a magnetic quantum oscillation), Thaas Theory by Koen Thas (a generalization of quantum theory), a THAAS test for auditory attention, and THAs (Theorems of Hyperarithmetic Analysis). 
Here are the specific theories and concepts that could be misconstrued as "Thaas theory":
De Haas–Van Alphen effect (DHVA): A phenomenon observed in materials where magnetization oscillates as a function of a magnetic field. This was discovered by W.J. de Haas and P.M. van Alphen, inspired by the Shubnikov–de Haas effect. 
General Quantum Theory by Koen Thas: This is a theoretical framework that extends classical quantum theory to a broader class of mathematical structures called division rings with involution. 
THAAS Test (Teste de Habilidade de Atenção Auditiva Sustentada): This is a test designed to evaluate sustained auditory attention, which is the ability to focus on sounds for a period of time. 
THAs (Theorems of Hyperarithmetic Analysis): These are principles in reverse mathematics and recursion-theoretic complexity, lying between iterations of the Turing jump and ATR$_0$.
